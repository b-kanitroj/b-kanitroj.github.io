CC 3: Debating. Use a visualisation in economic/policy commentary

Festival of Economics 2024 invites economists and experts to address critical economic questions, such as: What can we do to boost Britain’s productivity? and How is the recently elected Labour government performing?

Among the topics explored is social mobility. The panel titled "(Not) Moving on Up: How Can We Increase Social Mobility?" is chaired by Mehreen Khan (The Times) and features Lou Davies (Bedminster Down School), Lindsey Macmillan (University College London and the Centre for Education Policy and Equalising Opportunities), Suzanne Rolt (Arnolfini), and David Sturrock (Institute for Fiscal Studies). Together, they highlights key factors influencing social mobility, including education, housing and wealth inequality, labor market challenges, and gaps in policy. Discover more details here: (Not) Moving on up: How can we increase social mobility?

Here I have created two charts to support the discussion that Wealth, particularly in housing, has become more critical issues as the difficulty of entering the housing market has reinforced disparities.

The average age of first-time buyers shows an upward trend, reaching 33.5 years in 2022-23, highlighting growing challenges for young people entering the housing market.

To put it in perspective, the U.K. ranks 18th out of 27 countries in the percentage of households that own their homes as of 2022.
